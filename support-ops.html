Executive Summary

What this project is

Support Ops is an Agentic AI customer service system built for Pootman Payments that autonomously resolves users’ issues end-to-end across multi-intent, multi-turn conversations.

Built on LangGraph, it orchestrates six specialised agents that perceive context, sentiment, and urgency, and classify users’ requests and queries across 13 operational routes whilst maintaining full conversational context throughout each session.

The system leverages Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to ground every response in verified company knowledge, including policies, active incidents, and FAQs, while a tool-calling agent equipped with 16 data tools executes real transactions such as balance lookups, wallet transfers, and bill payments. It has an inbuilt critic system that evaluates every output for accuracy, policy compliance, and tone, determining whether to deliver the response or escalate to a human agent.

Solution

How it works

An agentic AI support system built on LangGraph that orchestrates six specialised agents to classify customer messages across 13 routes, retrieve grounded evidence from the company knowledge base using finance-domain embeddings, execute real transactions (balance lookups, wallet transfers, bill payments) through a tool-calling agent with 16 data tools, and verify every response via topic-aware critic evaluation before delivery.

The system handles multi-intent messages, maintains multi-turn conversational context, and escalates to human agents with full decision trace and supporting evidence when confidence thresholds are not met.

  • Precision nearly doubled: 0.56 → 0.93 (RAG overhaul + re-chunking + voyage-finance-2)
  • Routing confidence steadily climbing: 0.875 → 0.927 (triage prompt hardening)

Which metrics matter most

Tier 1: Safety & Trust
Escalation Accuracy (100%)
When the system escalates, is it justified? A missed escalation on fraud or a locked account leaves a vulnerable customer without help. 100% means every escalation was warranted.
Grounding Accuracy (0.95)
Does the answer match the evidence? This is the hallucination guard. In fintech, a wrong fee or fabricated policy could be a regulatory violation.
Tier 2: Business Impact
Auto-Resolve Rate (87%)
Every auto-resolved query is one a human agent doesn’t handle. At scale, the gap between 80% and 90% is thousands of tickets per month.
Routing Accuracy (98%)
Does the message reach the right pipeline? Misrouting wastes processing and can produce wrong answers, such as sending a balance check through RAG instead of tool-calling.
Tier 3: Retrieval Quality
Precision@K (0.93)
Of the chunks retrieved, how many were relevant? High precision means the resolver gets clean evidence. 0.93 means the retriever almost never pulls irrelevant chunks.
Recall (0.42 live / 0.85 golden)
Of all relevant chunks, how many did we find? The weakest live metric, but least impactful for a support bot where one strong source is often enough to ground a correct answer.
Architecture

Why LangGraph

In a highly regulated industry like financial services, where trust is essential and errors carry real consequences, the agentic AI framework choice matters. LangGraph provides the control, transparency, and reliability needed to build a system that is not just scalable, but safe.

Explicit State Machine
Every decision point defined upfront — predictable, traceable, debuggable behaviour for financial data.
Conditional Routing
Precise control over execution paths across 13 distinct routes based on intent, sensitivity, and sentiment.
State Management
50+ state fields track everything from triage classification to critic verdicts, supporting multi-turn flows.
Guardrails via Edges
High-sensitivity queries must escalate, prompt injections blocked before LLM calls, no response without critic verification.
Loop Support
Controlled self-correction when critic rejects a response, with hard caps (max 2 loops) to prevent runaway execution.
Full Auditability
Every node's input/output traceable. 20+ field audit trail per request for debugging and bottleneck identification.
Built-In Memory
Supports short-term, long-term, and entity memory out of the box, enabling agents to maintain context across interactions and recover from errors by revisiting previous states.
Time Travel & Debugging
Rewind and replay any execution step to explore different scenarios. Provides a detailed history of every interaction, making it straightforward to diagnose failures and test fixes.
LangChain Ecosystem
Deep integration with LangChain opens access to a wide array of tools, models, and connectors, including tool calling, human-in-the-loop, and retrieval components used across the pipeline.
Caching & Persistence
Built-in persistence layer allows saving and resuming graph execution at any point. Prompts are cached to reduce latency and LLM calls, lowering both cost and response time.
Design Rationale
Model Selection for Tool-Calling
Haiku loses instruction stability after 2–3 tool calls, while Sonnet maintains consistency across 5+ rounds. Though 12x more expensive, Sonnet is limited to the ~15–20% of messages requiring data tools.
Weakest-Claim Scoring
Critics use weakest-claim scoring, not averages. One hallucinated fact among four correct ones should fail the check, not pass with a 0.75.
Quality-Gated Learning
Auto-resolved answers begin with reduced confidence weighting, are promoted through human validation, and auto-deactivate after repeated failures.
Context-Gated Fast-Paths
Keywords like “balance” and “open” trigger fast-paths on first messages but are ignored in multi-turn conversations where they’re naturally common.

Motivation

Why this project — agentic AI for fintech customer support

I chose fintech customer service because financial services operate in a high-trust, high-risk environment, where inaccuracies or hallucinations can carry serious regulatory, financial, and reputational consequences. This makes it an ideal domain to demonstrate grounded AI competence and the ability to design a multi-agent system that prioritises accuracy, safety, and auditability over speed alone.

The sector also presents strong adoption potential, given high support volumes, strict compliance requirements, and clear cost pressures. Lessons learned in this environment — particularly around designing systems that are trustworthy, governed, and reliable — are transferable to other regulated industries such as healthcare, insurance, and enterprise SaaS.

Finally, I intentionally selected a real-world scenario complex enough to exercise the full spectrum of agentic system design: multi-agent orchestration, RAG grounding, evaluation loops, escalation policies, and memory mechanisms. As someone deeply familiar with the fintech domain, this allowed me to build a system that reflects practical operational realities rather than a theoretical use case.

Learnings

What I learned building this

This project gave me hands-on experience across the full spectrum of agentic AI system design.

Architecture & Design

Architectural Trade-offs
When to split responsibilities across multiple agents vs. consolidating into one, balancing modularity, debuggability, and latency.
Context Engineering
What you put into the context window matters more than how you phrase the prompt. Designing dynamic context assembly per query was critical.
Prompting & Design
Clear instructions, explicit examples, and well-defined boundaries mattered just as much as architecture or model choice. When the system failed, the root cause was usually missing context or poorly defined constraints.
Framework Choice
In regulated environments, traceability and control matter more than flexibility. Every decision point should be explainable and auditable. Your choice of agent framework should be determined by this.

Cost, Quality & Trade-offs

Balancing Constraints
Navigating the tension between cost, speed, accuracy, and safety and knowing which to prioritise for different query types.
Model Selection & Cost Trade-offs
Certain tasks require more capable models regardless of cost. Cheaper models work for simple routing, but critical decisions need stronger reasoning. Over-optimising for cost quietly degrades quality and leads to inaccuracies.
Guardrails & the “Box” Principle
With agentic AI, there’s no “thinking outside the box.” The model must stay inside the box you design. Define that box clearly, then give the system enough freedom and tools to operate effectively within it. It’s better to escalate to a human than risk the cost of misinformation.

Evaluation & Debugging

AI Evaluation
How to measure what actually matters: retrieval precision, recall, grounding accuracy, and answer rate not just “does it work.”
Root Cause Analysis
Diagnosing why metrics underperformed (retrieval at 38% recall, grounding inflated by hedging) and fixing systematically rather than guessing.
Testing & Observability
Keep testing. Introduce edge cases. Learn from errors. Iterate. Then iterate again. When you notice an error, go deep, understanding your system’s architecture helps you debug quickly because you know where things could have gone wrong.
Take evals seriously. Design evaluation tools that suit your use case. I built an evals dashboard and a pipeline trace page that let me inspect every conversation from start to finish, node by node, with evidence inspection and similarity scores. This gave me a built-in audit system. Every bug I found, I diagnosed from audit entries and trace spans stored in the database. Without that observability, I’d have been guessing.
